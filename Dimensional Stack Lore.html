4.1:: Chapter 1

About 300,000 years ago, Outzschcrad split off from his parent in a mitosis. Because Endbeings inherit all knowledge of their parent at the time of birth, there are no specific relationships between parents and children, so Outzschcrad took their starter kit and left the house to find work. They immediately noticed the extreme curvature of the world. They lived in the S3xE1 realm, where three dimensions looped like the surface of a 4-dimensional hypersphere (also known as a glome), while the Euclidean direction, which was horizontal, extended infinitely. The glome was divided into 16 portions corresponding to individual cells of a 2x2x2x2 array of tesseracts, with sidewalks in between them. One of them specifically held a 6x6 square grid of train tracks, analogous to the parallel tracks on Earth's train stations. These tracks seemed to stretch infinitely, connecting the whole world together. The other 15 parts held buildings of arbitrary length but constant width, height, and trength. Little boxes were nailed to the ground at regular intervals, containing incredibly dense material to generate gravity so that the correct direction was up, though this didn't fix the slight inconvenience that one might occasionally have to rotate themselves to counteract a phenomenon called holonomy, a natural rotation you acquire while merely traveling. The whole dimension was around 68 meters wide, tall, and trong. It would feel very cramped to most people, but it fostered close, friendly relationships with one's neighbors. Outzschcrad studied their own duplicated mind for knowledge of what job they would like. They, again, had all knowledge acquired by their parent available to them, but crucially, they had their own personality. They were reliable, a master of wisecracker humor, and more than a little eccentric. Outzschcrad decided they would be most fulfilled rolling their own, being the CEO of their own company. But what to sell? Searching for a bit, Outzschcrad remembered inter-dimensional travel was popular. If one was a species in which the method of separating and retethering consciousness was known, such as Endbeings and Phykommatons, one could travel between worlds of varying topologies. For Endbeings, it was especially easy, since given the species' lack of specialization, almost any topology with the same number of dimensions was accessible. But for topologies a species did not support, you would need to transfer your consciousness into a robot built for the destination topology, and preserve the body to come back to later. Most robots made this way integrated the consciousness through the entire robot body, to make it easier to develop the mind-body interface. But this made the robots much harder are more expensive to modify or swap out. Outzschcrad had an idea, that the head could contain everything needed for the mind and its enrichment, and everything else as an accessory for movement, grasping, and aesthetic appeal. The Euclidean topology was the biggest center of business out of all 4-dimensional realms, so Outzschcrad went to the free portal station and stepped into E4. Gxeostlva! This is one spacious place!

  • A dimension is a perpendicular dimension of space.   • A Riemannian manifold is a combination of a manifold that defines how a space loops back on itself, and a metric which defined how such a space is curved (meaning that moving around warps your perspective and distances are defined weirdly).

5.1.2:: Geometric structure

The Stack is technically one giant manifold with an infinite (specifically ℵ0) number of dimensions, hosted by a deity named Thynge. ℵ0 of them are arrangement dimensions, and another aleph-null are space/time dimensions. All of this updates in discrete intervals in meta-time, which "unravels" multiple dimensions of time and other weird things such as time travel (time travel specifically updates everything after it in just one instant, doing any arbitrarily large amount of computation in the same amount of time to do so.) In the latter case, most paradoxes have a defined outcome, for example the bootstrap paradox can't happen and the grandfather paradox redefines the killer's descent and genetics to someone else.

So how many Riemannian manifolds are there? Well, there are ℶ1 real numbers and ℶ2 functions on real numbers, and it's still ℶ2 even if there are multiple inputs or outputs. However, Riemannian manifolds are defined as being smooth, meaning infinitely differentiable, which implies that the function is continuous. And the number of continuous functions on real numbers is beth-one [https://math.stackexchange.com/questions/2127258/why-is-the-set-of-all-continuous-functions-of-size-beth-one]. The linked proof seemingly should extend to any finite number of inputs and outputs (though I'm too lazy to prove it). The manifold part of Riemannian manifolds should be expressible as a smooth parametric equation, and the metric is a smooth function on that parametric equation's parameters to a matrix of real numbers. Both of these have cardinality beth-one, so their tuple should also. Thus the cardinality of finite-dimensional Riemannian manifolds should be ℶ1.

With ℵ0 dimensions of space, it's possible to form a set of all points with only coordinates 0 and 1. This set has cardinality ℶ1, as it can be mapped to real numbers between 0 and 1 in binary. Thus every point can be mapped to a Riemannian manifold with exactly enough. In fact every point can be mapped to a pair, even a triplet or arbitrarily large finite tuple of Riemannian manifolds and still have exactly enough.

The Stack uses triplets of Riemannian manifolds without boundaries, the first being the space, the second being time (which would be a ray in our universe), and the third being the "rubber-band present", according to a concept called circular or manifold present, invented by Tessimal, in which every object behaves like a straight rubber band crossing the present manifold in the product of the space and the present ( this [https://tessimal.itch.io/circular-present-physics-simulator] is a simulation of space E2 and present E1). The way time manifolds other than E1 work is essentially that time flows according to an expanding sphere in the manifold, that is, all points distance D away from a chosen center point, where D is the time that has passed, represented as a real-valued quantity of irrelevant units. If a point on this time sphere reaches a point that is already occupied by a previous instant, it is overwritten by some sort of combination of the old and new states, and the way they combine is not well-understood.

These hypercube-like points are mapped to are "one-half" of the ℵ0 dimensions in the stack's manifold (though that isn't really a formal mathematical notion in infinite contexts, more a linguistic convenience). The other half are the dimensions the space x time x rubber-band present manifolds are embedded into. The choice of where space, time, and present are in these embeddings varies, but every permutation of the three is included separately. The forces and mechanics that apply inside manifolds technically apply to the aether between them too, and sometimes one manifold can physically affect other manifolds that are very close.

If two manifolds differ by an infinite number of these hypercube coordinates, they are inaccessible from each other via field forces and wormhole travel, but they can still be accessed via quasi-astral projection to a robot or divine intervention by Thynge. However, all triplets of computable manifolds (of which there are a countable amount) are fully accessible, so this isn't a huge problem.

5.1.3:: The canonical definition of consciousness for the Stack

The basic concepts of consciousness/sapience are as follows:

  1. There is no such thing as continuity. Your experience is merely the one-by-one loading of present instants into memory, and the sensible progression from very recent memory to the present creates the illusion of a continuous experience of the world. In a sense, a new instance of you is created many times per second just to make a decision and then immediately killed; your brain disregards this as it is pointless.
  2. What defines you separately from another entity is: If the memories of the other entity replaced yours while you remain in the same present situation, would you be suspicious that their memories belonged to you? If yes, you and the other are different people. This is highly subjective to how one's mind works. In the case of the beings of the Stack, multiple sequences of memories can converge on one present without violating this, as long as all the sequences all diverge from one past point. This allows for the "merge" operation to exist.
  3. An individual can never truly stop existing, however there can be an arbitrary number of perspectives on who counts as a given individual.

Thus:

  • If you get amnesia or your memory is otherwise wiped, you are a new person from then on. (To adhere to rule three, which specific to the Stack, amnesia typically behaves as a branch option where the new branch takes over the old body and the old stream of sapience is emitted as a math agent.)
  • You rarely ever change identities throughout your life; even if you are immortal, you only become a new individual when you become so different that your old and new selves are irreconcilable. (In the Stack, this just doesn't happen.)
  • If you get cloned exactly during teleportation (a.k.a. forking), the distinct entity of you lingers a bit before forking into two distinct beings. (Both of which are valid perspectives on who counts as you, but they don't count as each other, meaning being the same entity is not transitive.)
  • If teleportation works properly, you remain you. Your continuity doesn't get broken as it never existed in the first place. We live on a high enough level that it is completely irrelevant that the departing you died; that instance of consciousness would have died either way upon being converted to memory.

There are three operations in the stack that can be applied to an existing stream of sapience:

  • A branch creates a new stream of sapience, copied from the old, but with a new identity. All memories remain intact, but they must be deliberately and forcefully accessed and queried rather than remembered, creating an intrinsic sense that these are not their memories. A new personality also gets attached to the new stream. One common method by which this can occur is mitosis.
  • A fork splits a stream of sapience into two or more exact copies, all of which are equally the previous identity. A fork creates a "debt" for a corresponding merge, and the longer this debt goes unpaid by their sense of time, the more mentally unstable and distant from each other the forks get, until eventually they dissociate into entirely separate individuals with semi-randomized personalities and become unable to merge (this process also emits a math agent copy of the pre-forked stream separate from all forks and continuing where they left off pre-fork). Forks also gain an intrinsic, overpowering desire/instinct to merge with each other as soon as they in any way interact with or perceive one another, even to the point they'd sacrifice their loved ones if they somehow get in the way (though merely knowing they exist or thinking about them is thankfully not enough to trigger this). It is dangerous and ill-advised to be around a stranded fork that has contacted or seen its counterparts.

    Problematic forks are rare. The main source of them are differing timelines of a individual who is currently perceiving local time rather than meta-time, which occurs nearly constantly whenever a math agent affects the past in any way. These timelines would need to do crazy multi-dimensional time hijinx to able to contact or perceive each other at all, and will merge immediately when all of them are dead and they become a math agent able to perceive meta-time. However, one method of creating dangerous forks is when a being of a given dimension of rubber-band present manifold travels to a world with a rubber-band present manifold of a higher dimension. When this occurs, the being will automatically fork into a grid-like series of embeddings of their rubber-band present manifold into the higher one. All of them are thus in constant contact until they leave. While leaving will normally merge them, it's also possible for them to each leave into separate worlds, in which case they will hunt each other down, which is dangerous for anyone involved.
  • A merge happens between members of a previous fork that haven't dissociated yet. This merge doesn't have to involve every member, and members that don't get merged initially can still be merged with an already existing conglomerate, as long as everyone involved shares a common forked ancestor with no branches in between. The merge combines all forks' memories into one mind and one stream of sapience containing divergent paths of memories leading up to the present.

    Two common methods achieving a merge with arbitrary circumstances are if forks perceive meta-time (by death or quasi-astral projection), or if the brains of forks portal or teleport so that their interiors intersect (this method causes deformities that must be corrected by a combination of brain surgery, digestive tract surgery or removal, and reconfiguration). 5.1.7:: The experience of multiple dimensions of time

The real present of worlds with a manifold of time (not to be confused with the present manifold for the rubberband effects) is an expanding sphere, with any revisited points having the past and present combined. How this is experienced by people living in it is complicated.

First, the general causality. As the sphere expands, stuff happens and affects more distant areas on the manifold. This is the simple, roughly linear dimension of causality. The others are the latitude, longitude, etc. of the expanding sphere. They can each be expressed as a number between 0 and 2π. Imagine all these together make a square, cube, or higher equivalent. The point at 0,0 is the base point where causality starts. From there, in each instant of meta-time, causality expands along an expanding subcube anchored to 0,0 that grows. In the square version of this, the two edges work like two continuums of present instants. Each "layer" that adds on to the square performs causality both all the way along the edge and one iteration step perpendicular, as well as implementing causality with the corresponding instants on the spherical layer below. Free will and non-determinism apply here; they can cause different things to happen on different regions of the edge. Along the edges is a flow of time, but it's not necessarily continuous as things can abruptly happen to continue the other axis' causality properly. In the cube version of this, each of the three relevant faces act like the square within themselves, performing causality as a mini version of the expanding square on the face, and doing one iteration step perpendicular to the whole face. Higher dimensions do this recursively. All of this is calculated in one instant of meta-time. When a new meta-time instant starts computing, one iteration of causality is run on the 0,0,0... point. Also, as you approach 2π on any given axis, all factors slowly start being ignored, and everything perfectly and automatically conspires to match 0 on the axis. This is quite steep and doesn't take up much area, but when any axis is about 6.19 and after, free will is not really a thing anymore. Finally, as the sphere grows, the amount of events that can happen in one radian grows steadily, so a more Euclidean version of the time manifold could be described as a wrapping hypercubic pyramid that just keeps growing downwards rather than having a base.

The creation of streams of sapience in a given world is synchronized across the sphere, and so are the forks, branches, and merges of anyone born in that sphere. (This means that all pieces of a person are born from the same parent individual). Death resets this; if you are reborn into a new world, you gain that world's present sphere.

The conscious experience of someone within the world is a dense spread of choices and experiences distributed across the sphere, and this experience layout is carried with them even if they enter a world with a different timespace. The experience might not be continuous due to abrupt changes propogating from other axes of time, but the choices they can make only apply in a fine grid that creates new points to fill gaps as the sphere expands. Choices, of course, propogate causally to themselves (for example, if you raise your arm, all axes of future will have your arm raised, unless it was somehow lowered by another one of the futures' relative pasts). Someone with two dimensions of time can travel to thousands of worlds at once, in parallel, but some of the surrounding area on their time sphere comes along for the ride sitting in an empty void. It also leads to something that might be considered a lot weirder. Time manifolds cannot have boundaries, so unlike our universe where time is a ray, one-dimensional time must be either a weird, constantly recombining circle, or a full bi-directional line. Either way, you have exactly two, entirely disjoint experiences. On the line specifically, this means everyone lives two lives at once, and are aware of both of them. These lives differ a lot and it is practically impossible to have them be anything like each other in terms of lifestyle, but their personalities often update in tandem and either can draw on knowledge from both. At the level of the conscious mind, it would feel like playing two video games simultaneously without juggling them or switching between them at all. Most senses have two copies, apart from those that only function in only one life. Uncovesseltuxe also lives the life of a low-level internet service provider employee named Typhumebiek, and has said he is "not proud of some things I've done under that sheltered name", though this quote is recorded from 4000 BCE human time and may no longer be accurate.

5.1.8:: Experience of the mind The experience within the mind of a stream of sapience in the stack varies a lot by species, but a standard set of "senses" exist. Streams of sapience don't really have a defined storage, input, or output limit on what they can remember, experience, and control. They can definitely be overwhelmed, but their senses don't "give up" if they have too many eyes or something. In fact, they're capable of processing uncountably infinite quantities of data points near effortlessly.
  • An intrinsic, infinite, fully comprehensive view of every field at every point of the Stack. For most creatures, it isn't experienced except as a math agent or when doing quasi-astral projection. For Thyngeoids, it is always present.
  • A set of multi-dimensional viewports that get occupied by eyes and are perceived as local sight. These usually store a field of color vectors. The experience of this sense is highly determined by the brain, which does not typically reconfigure; however, the rods and cones in their eyes do reconfigure. This results in an actually interesting result to the classic question "Is your red the same as my red?": There's no way of knowing the answer to this question specifically, but from one form to the next, the answer is a definite NO. In fact, if you base the measurements on the initial perception of the color spectrum, you can definitively and accurately find exactly which colors switched around.

    The Omnigeon brain is hardwired for trichromacy, but when eyes have additional cone types, it will compensate by adding additional viewports to represent new stuff as "impossible colors" from color mismatch between eyes, except it may be more than just two eyes. Phykommatons and Divergials are also trichromats, but Hyperpseudoquasithretxramorphs can directly perceive tetrachromatic colors as hues within a planar rainbow (also, in a similar way to how yellow is pretty different from red and green even though it's a combination of the two, they treat the new cone type as a disambiguator between multiple separate linear rainbows, usually 6 or 7).
  • A set of hearing senses, typically one per ear. They automatically do spectral analysis to perceive multiple pitches rather than a complex wave (this is the same as in humans, and it's why music is possible at all).
  • Similar deal to the above, there's one sense of smell per nose and one sense of taste per mouth.
  • A sense of touch, pressure, temperature, pain, moisture, and radiation across the surface of their skin.
  • Hyper-advanced versions of balance and proprioception that can quickly adapt to any body configuration.
  • A very weak and undetailed conscious knowledge of one's own form, without needing to perceive oneself. It's not even close to good enough to describe what color you are, what your face looks like, etc., but it's enough to immediately know what archetype you are, your basic limb types and arrangements, or if you have extra heads or snake hair.
  • Immediate and adaptive knowledge and use of walking and/or swimming patterns for arbitrary limb type and arrangement. However, new trinsfurs take a few hours or days to acquire this.
  • A lingering, strange sensation that occurs when one's chance of reconfiguration is high and immediate. It's not comparable to much else but it mentally falls in line with the "touch" senses. A few very inaccurate descriptions that approximate this feeling in human terms:
    • "It's like if your skin rapidly changed consistencies while sharply dividing and recombining to raised bumps arranged in different tilings."
    • "It's like if your entire body could feel a pulse all at once where having a severe heart palpitation. But like, in a pleasurable way."
    • "It's like if your skin was a shifting mass of tectonic plates separated by magma, with small eruptions throughout."
    • "It's like if you knew perfectly well you had a pair of legs, and you could use them just fine, but all of your touch-related senses indicated that you had a lightly buttered mermaid tail which was involuntarily curving, raising, and slapping the ground, and were currently sitting. Everything in this analogy feels genuine, except the touch of the ground, in which it feels like the tail has slightly fallen asleep, but not very heavily."
  • A manually queryable database of memories from everyone you are descended from.

5.2.1:: Quatrammotile and descendants
Quatrammotile (native name Q'lot'rov'matoev'lu) is a language spoken by Omnigeons in the E4 dimension. It is part of the Toroidal language family.
5.2.1.1:: Phonology

There's also the uvular trill /ʁ/, romanized G.

However, none of these phonemes are exact, and are to be approximated as close as possible.

5.2.1.2:: Phonotactics

Syllables can be V, VC, CV, or CVC. V can have an arbitrary number of individual vowel sounds in a diphthong, triphthong, etc. There are no rules for what consonants can start and end words.

5.2.1.3:: Orthography

Quatrammotile is written on 3D paper using 2D manifolds. Its script is an abugida, meaning it notates vowels with diacritics. This script also juxtaposes the phonemes in various situations for uvular plosivization, and R, L, M, N, and Agma coloring.

Consonants:

  • P - Boy's surface
  • T - Kushner-Bryant parameterization of Boy's surface
  • Q - Two-holed torus
  • F - Extruded sine graph portion
  • V - Extruded sine graph rotated 90 degrees in the xz plane
  • Th - Concentric spheres
  • Tx - Horn torus
  • G - Self-intersecting Klein bottle

Modifiers:

  • Uvularly plosivizing the plosives is done by putting them inside a cylinder.
  • R-coloring consonants puts them on a torus like a wedding ring.
  • L-coloring consonants puts them inside the hole of a torus.
  • M-coloring fricatives puts two squares at a right angle on the bottom right wint
  • N-coloring fricatives does so on the bottom right zant,
  • Agma-coloring fricatives does so on the bottom left zant.

The five vowels are notated with 3D platonic solid-shaped diacritics.

  • E - tetrahedron
  • O - octahedron
  • A - cube
  • W - dodecahedron
  • U - icosahedron

If the consonant doesn't have a vowel after it, such as at the end of a syllable, it's just empty. There can also be some diphthongs, in this case, stack the platonic solids bottom to top in chronological order. When vowels start a word, a flat heptagon is used as a placeholder for the diacritics.

Punctuation marks are Archimedian solids:

  • The truncated tetrahedron marks the end of a factual sentence.
  • The cuboctahedron marks the end of an opinionated sentence.
  • The icosidodecahedron marks the end of a lie, though for fiction where the reader shouldn't know the truth yet, the truncated tetrahedron or cuboctahedron is used instead, depending on whether the statement is an opinion.
  • The small rhombicuboctahedron marks a question.
  • The great rhombicuboctahedron marks an exclamation.
  • The small icosidodecahedron marks a command.
  • The great icosidodecahedron marks either an insult or a compliment. Essentially any statement that affirms opinions about another person.
  • The truncated cube marks a pause, but isn't exactly like a comma since it doesn't have any mandatory positions and doesn't have any restrictions are where it's allowed to go when included.
  • The truncated octahedron separates two sentences, but establishes the second as strictly in the context of the first (closest equivalent is a colon).
  • The truncated dodecahedron is used as a self-interruption
  • The truncated icosahedron is essentially a quotation mark. You have to put Platonic, Kepler-Poinsot, uniform, uniform dual (the infinitely large uniform duals can be cut off at any reasonable radius), Johnson, or Johnson dual diacritics above it to mark one or more marks as being the same quote. The truncated icosahedron without a diacritic marks all currently used quote diacritics empty, while putting the diacritic below only empties that one. There's a healthy supply of 5+4+66*2+92*2=325 available quotes, but in extremely rare cases where that isn't enough, you can stack the diacritics, with the below form being upside down in order (since it's applied in the same order but in a different direction)
  • The snub cube's two chiral forms are used as parenthesis, with a similar diacritical system to truncated icosahedra, except that the diacritics are more of a visual aid than anything and what really matters is that the parentheses match. The snub cube without diacritics sets the chirality of the open bracket, which may be switched.
  • The snub dodecahedron's two chiral forms are used mostly the same as the snub cube, but the snub cube is general-purpose and the snub dodecahedron is for technical or math notation (like curly brackets or square brackets).

5.2.1.4:: Grammar

Word order is IVOS, where I stands for the indirect object, which must always be present. If there is no indirect object, you just say "space" or "time" depending on whether your action is more space-descriptive or more time-descriptive, but this is pretty loose and is left up to the speaker.

There are seven noun classes:

  • Pointlike
  • Linear
  • Sheetlike
  • Boxlike
  • Tesseractive
  • Supertesseractive
  • Temporal

Nouns don't belong to a noun class, rather they get assigned arbitrarily, sometimes changing the meaning, other times conveying extra information or emphasis.

For example, past, present, and future also mean all of the relative directions like forward, backward, up, down, left, right, wint, zant, etc. depending on what noun class they're assigned to.

5.2.1.6:: Quatrammotilization

When loaning words into Quatrammotile from Earth languages, the following rules apply.

For consonants:

  • /k/ becomes Q, while /g/ becomes Q'L

Consecutive pairs of consonants that don't happen on syllable boundaries get separated with U.

Vowels are somewhat arbitrary.

5.2.1.10:: Alternate orthographies
5.2.1.10.1:: Latin orthography

For 3D creatures, the Latin orthography is the standard. It treats uvular plosivized consonants and liquid/nasal-colored consonants as layered consonants, which is close, but not exactly right as they could be pronounced other ways if they sound similar, and even layering the audio is a crude approximation. However, layered consonants is possibly easier to understand than "use arbitrary methods to make a consonant sound like a hybrid of two consonants".

  • /ptq/ are Latinized as P/p, T/t, and Q/q.
  • /⌺/ (the orthoplex plosive) is Latinized as D/d, though this is not well-representative of the sound by any means.
  • /fvθð/ are Latinized as F/f, V/v, Th/th, and Tx/tx. H and X do not occur on their own (though H may also mean Agma-colored if preceded by an apostrophe), so this is unambiguous.
  • /ʁ/ (the uvular trill) is Latinized as G/g, though like D/d this is not representative of the sound.
    • Gr/gr is used to represent a degeneticized version with some other sound, such as the human "French R" approximation. Uncovesseltuxe's 3D team uses this approach instead of the X-waved arms.
    • Gx/gx is used to represent a degeneticized version with the crossed-appendage gesture.
  • /ɛɒouʊ/ are Latinized as E/e, O/o, A/a, W/w, U/u. A and O can be quite confusing, as they're often the exact switch of what you would expect (O is like "a" in father (or various similar vowels, the exact quality doesn't really matter in this language) while A is like "o" in grow).
  • Uvularly plosivized consonants are represented with the base consonant followed by 'q
  • R, L, M, N, and Agma-colored consonants are represented by taking the base consonant followed by 'r, 'l, 'm, 'n, or 'h respectively. These letters do not occur on their own, other than H in the digraph TH.

The Latin orthography was originally going to be all uppercase, but that looked absolutely terrible so it was changed to behave like English's case system.

5.2.1.10.2:: Greek orthography

While the Latin orthography is standard, Quatrammotile and its Toroidal language family have some minimal influence from both Ancient and Modern Greek due to a live broadcast of the simulated E3 world with lots of Toroidal-family-speaking listeners, with Greece being a popular viewing location due to its aesthetics and its role in E3 mathematics. So, a Greek orthography was created.

  • /ptq⌺/ are represented as π, τ, κ, ψ (Pi, Tau, Kappa, Psi).
    • Uvularly plosivizing these consonants doubles them.
  • /fvθð/ are represented as φπ, φβ, θπ, θβ (Phi and Theta, using Pi and Beta to indicate voiceless/voiced)
  • R, L, M, N, and Agma coloring are done by following the consonant with ρ, λ, μ, ν, γ (Rho, Lambda, Mu, Nu, Gamma) respectively
  • The uvular trill is ξ (Xi) when fully realized, χξ (Chi Xi) when encoded with a crossed-appendage gesture, and χχ (Chi Chi) when approximated vocally.
  • /ɛɒouʊ/ are represented as ε, ο, ω, α, υ (Epsilon, Omicron, Omega, Alpha, Upsilon). Once again a vowel gets a weird job, this time being Alpha for /u/ since we're out of good vowel letters.

5.2.1.14.2.4:: Native writing system
5.2.1.14.3:: Grammar

Word order in Quatrammotile is IVOS, where I is the indirect object. Unlike Quatrammotile 1.0, I and O can be omitted. However, the suffix -pren must be added to the verb if I is deleted, or -prom if I and O are deleted.

Sentences can also contain extra parallel sentences at the end to tack on intention and emotion. Intention is preceded by "cheqeth", while emotion is preceded by "lxaeth". These must always come after the main sentence. If "cheqeth" or "lxaeth" start a sentence, it just states your intentions and emotions with no imparted knowledge. REMEMBER: The main sentence carries over only knowledge, and it's improper to derive anything else from it, including why they might have said it, possible reasons for their word choice, emotion in their tone of voice, etc. It may however contain and depend on context as long as any intentional or emotional context was stated during the conversation.

The rule of nesting sentences within nouns to refer to the subject of the sentence has now been removed, as I'm worried about it potentially introducing written ambiguity. Grammatical parentheses using tepav and cepaq still apply, and this is the only way to do any recursion at all.

5.2.1.14.5:: Word definition/reassignment

One problem with Quatrammotile is that the words are just too dang long! While I don't have a great solution to that, I do know that defining variables is good for code golfing.

So, new grammatical construct! Any phrase that can be put inside tepav/depaq brackets can now be defined as a word for the remainder of the conversation.

The construct is merely tacked on to an existing use of tepav and depaq, as:
  ephoq?elh [new word] tepav [phrase] depaq[suffixes]

The new word overwrites any previous definitions of a word, which can be a neat rhetorical technique (redefine the word for "war" as "mindless struggle for control freaks" then use the latter exactly how you would use the former).

5.2.2:: Sessive

Sessive is the primary language spoken by residents of E6. It operates on the philosophy that 1. Intentions should always be clear, and 2. As much should be left to context as possible. It is also a Toroidal language very distant from Quatrammotile, which is evident in its number system.

The grammar of a sentence consists of:
- A statement of intention.
- Zero or more adjectives, which may modify each other in reverse polish notation.

5.2.3:: Divergic
5.2.3.1:: Orthography

A sentence starts and ends with inward facing angle brackets ">I went to the beach today<". These represent "genetic taps" which I will get into later. A sentence is not composed in the usual word-like structure though.

Rather, a sentence begins with two arbitrary symbols, representing a continuing vowel and a terminating vowel, followed by a space. Then, a list of more arbitrary symbols, called consonants, alternating with the continuing vowel, with the last one having a terminating vowel.

For example: ">ae bacadafagahe ...<"

What follows that weird alternating pattern is more alternating patterns, using the "consonants" already defined arbitrarily, alternating with the continuing vowel with the last one using the terminating vowel.

A few examples:

  • >io kiRi?i%o Ri%i%i?o kikiRi?i?o<
  • >@* H@J@K@G@W@L* J@J@G@H@L* K@W* L@L@J@H*<
  • >ea sepereteSa SeSepeteta teta peSepesa<

Question marks and exclamations have no use as the speakers aren't necessarily expressive enough, and the following equivalence is present:

  • Comma: ^
  • Colon: |
  • Semicolon: ][
5.2.3.2:: Biology/phonology

This language is spoken by beings living in geometric hyperbolic space, either in 3 or 4 dimensions. Their biology is really different from humans, and therefore their mouths produce totally different sounds.

First of all, all of these people, known as Divergials, have the ability to pronounce a phoneme known as the "genetic tap", which completely reforms their entire body. Unlike the related cross-topological Endbeings, these forms have a consistent structure, with specific mouthpart arrangements.

Unfortunately these arrangements still vary a lot.

Divergials, rather than having teeth, a velum, an uvula, etc., have a random amount of closures along with lipless mouth apertures that block most sound. These closures are uniform hyperbolic tilings around a vertex, which can smash together, fitting perfectly and creating a metal clank sound that varies by material, which tiling, and how far back the closure is. In their mouths there are glands that expand or contract the natural curvature to just the right amount for each tiling. These were originally evolved as an equivalent to teeth.

This has two consequences. One, the curvature glands require a pre-existing curvature of space to function, meaning in topologies without a hyperbolic curvature in at least one direction, they can't speak and can't eat solid foods. In topologies with partial hyperbolic curvature, such as H2 x E2 or ~SL(2, R), they have to orient their heads in the right direction. So they need aid outside of their homeworld, unlike the more adaptable Endbeings.

Another consequence is that, between speakers, the consonants will nearly always be completely different, though they usually sound like metal clanks in some form. However, they will always have the genetic tap and at least two different sounds capable without closure, called vowels, though these sounds are still different between speakers. The language thus cannot be dependant on exact phonology.

Phonology-independant meaning

So how do you represent meaning with only one consistent sound, that also happens to completely change all other sounds? Well, here's the system.

  • Start a sentence with a genetic tap.
  • Pronounce two sounds as "vowels", one to show that a word continues and one to terminate a word.
  • Pronounce any number of sounds as consonants, each one followed by a continuing vowel except for the last consonant which is followed by a terminating vowel.
  • Use those consonants as a base of numbers, and assign meaning to the numbers.
  • Pronounce a series of numbers, using the terminating vowels to decide the boundaries between numbers.
  • End the sentence with another genetic tap.

This system means that base conversion is stored in the language part of Divergials' brains, which makes them super good and fast at it.

Numbers to words

Hyperbolese uses the negative base of the number of listed consonants, which means all integers both positive and negative can be represented without an additional negative sign.

Words start with a prefix as a semantic type. They're divided into three categories:

  • Positive composite numbers greater than 1 denote tables of possible words. They're followed by two more numbers denoting an index in the table. These tables are usually gradual in nature, allowing you to specify numbers outside the table for more exaggerated meaning, or fractions using built-up numbers for intermediate meaning.
  • Positive prime numbers, 1, and 0 denote grammatical structures or other things that aren't just a word. They can take an arbitrary or varying amount of arguments.
  • Negative numbers are for private use, such as jargon, slang, or inside jokes or lingo. Some of these are more standard than others, but the language's grammar doesn't care about these and they're essentially a free-for-all.
5.2.3.3:: Proper nouns and Macrelydve's IPA Extension

Hyperbolese usually just uses a giant non-gradual table of proper nouns where you can claim a spot in an online registry. Unfortunately, this means English proper nouns don't have a great way of being represented in standard Hyperbolese.

Meet Macrelydve (their real name is 90139, -600148 on the proper name table but we'll call them by his chosen human name). They're a friend of the team of Endbeings who run our simulated world as a proof that Euclidean 3D beings can be intelligent, and also happen to be a Divergial. As such, they needed a nice way to encode proper nouns of humanity into their language. Enter, the IPA, short for Interdimensional Phonetic Array. Macrelydve's system uses -324 as the prefix, followed by two coordinates, much like a semantic table. Get it, because they're trying to move us from dimensions three to four?

5.2.3.5:: Non-composite lexicon (grammatical things)

0 - conjugation

0 can be followed by:

  • 0, then a noun case, then a noun class, then a noun number, and then a noun. Conjugates a noun. The whole structure can be considered a noun, but cannot be used in this structure. Newer dialects may omit the case and class.
  • 1, then a verb tense, then a verb mood, then the subject, direct object, and indirect object, the noun classes of the three, and then a verb.   • 3, followed by a part of speech identifier. Represents a null word that carries no meaning but holds a part of speech. Null nouns can have arbitrary class, which carries no meaning.

  • -2, takes a word, makes its negation

1 - modification

Takes in two part of speech identifiers as in grammar specifier 0, and two words, modifies the first word with the second, returning a phrase of the first type.

2 - pronouns

Takes in a noun class, number, and case, and a person number.

The person number has similar things to 1st, 2nd, and 3rd person, which are done with 3rd, 4th, and 5th person, but also a few others:

  • Person 2, the topic itself
  • Person 1, that which contains the topic
  • Person 0, for a deity (only used by religious Divergials)

3 - Noun phrase recursion

Takes in a partial sentence, and turns it into a noun phrase which specifies the subject(s) where the sentence is true.

5 - Word extensions

Followed by a number and additional arguments:

  • 0, takes a number, forms a literal number
  • 1, takes two numbers as a numerator and denominator, forms a fraction. These can be used as numbers to index tables with 5 4.
  • 2, takes in three numbers, a, b, and c. Makes a binary floating point number with a before the point, b extra zeros after the point, and c after all of the zeros. These can be used as numbers to index tables with 5 4.
  • 3, takes in two numbers, indexes a table of proper nouns
  • 4, followed by a table number and two numbers formed with 5 0, 5 1, or 5 2. Indexes the table gradually, interpolating between integers.

5.3:: Species of the Stack
5.3.1:: Omnigeons

An Omnigeon (short for N-dimensional being) is a sapient (except in 2D, under intense debate for 3D) creature that inhabits all universes in The Stack except for E0 and E1 where no life can exist.

Omnigeons don't look very alike, even in the same topology. This is because of their intense sensitivity to radiation which can cause their appearances to spontaneously, drastically, and irreversibly rearrange. An individual appearance is known as a "form". While forms can be almost anything in theory, they almost always fit into one of a few archetypes:

5.3.1.1:: Archetypes
5.3.1.1.1:: Brachiocrus, a mix of draconic features, human features, and random mutations, with varying numbers of arms and legs

Brachiocrus is the name of the most common archetype of Omnigeon forms.

Brachiocri are the most humanoid out of the forms, the rest being weird arrangements of eyes, wings, rings, and other parts.

They have a varying number of arms, legs, and eyes, which can be arranged in many ways, such as the vertices of a symmetrical polytope. Sometimes the legs will instead be a serpent-like tail. However, it's extremely rare for a Brachiocrus to have only one leg or arm, though this can happen sometimes with the eyes. Usually Brachiocrus have bilateral symmetry, but they can sometimes have trilateral, quadrilateral, quintilateral, or even further symmetries.

Brachiocrus also can have varying mutations such as Triton-style ears or asymmetrically forked mouths (shaped like a sideways Y) [this is not even close to an exhaustive list].

Facial structure usually varies between draconic and humanlike, but can sometimes take on other forms such as wolflike or catlike, or occasionally even have something close to an LED display made out of natural cells (called a Divergial facial structure due to its similarity to Divergials.).

About 60% of forms are Brachiocric.

5.3.1.1.2:: Symbolacron, one central core polytope surrounded by duoprisms of characters in writing systems, typically Greek

Symbolacron is the name of the second-most common archetype of Omnigeon forms.

Symbolacra have a central polytope as opposed to the central eye of an Arraphim. The polytope is known as the core, and is always symmetrical to a certain degree, but usually is regular, uniform, or the dual of a uniform. The polytope is surrounded by a symmetrical arrangement of irregular multiprisms pointing outwards, known as "glyphs". In odd dimensions, the glyphs are two side-by-side flat multiprisms of the previous dimension (technically slightly extruded into the new dimension since nothing is completely flat in any dimension). The 2D components of these multiprisms are typically Greek letters in serif or sans-serif forms, with both cases equally likely, typically the same two letters for all duoprisms. Occasionally other human scripts can happen though. It seems human writing systems in E3/Tuxe were influenced by the simulation overseers to match the Symbolacra.

The core has similar qualities to that of the Toriphan archetype. One facet opens into a mouth/vocal tract, while the other opens into the waste-expelling end, and the core contains all of the internal organs.

Also similar to Toriphans, the rings are bound to the core by an invisible, incredibly strong force. The force does not block things from passing between the rings and the core, nor does such an occurence interrupt the force or harm the Omnigeon.

Symbolacra have telekinesis for picking up and using objects. For more precision, each glyph has a paltry few dozen millimeters of positional and angular leeway from its typical orientation, which can be controlled like an arm. This is likely why the glyphs tend to be letter-like, with lots of hooks and bends for grip. As for walking, they just roll in any direction in any subset of the six rotational planes, though double-rotation takes more energy than single-rotation since it requires both propulsion engines (a pair of core organs) to be activated rather than just one.

It's possible, though rare, for a Symbolacron form to have be resistant to radiation, similar to most Arraphim forms.

Symbolacra lack vision organs and instead have an intrinsic sight sense that sees all angles around them as if it were projected to the inside of a hypersphere.

About 30% of forms are Symbolacronic.

5.3.1.1.2.1:: Trivia

5.3.1.1.3:: Toriphan, featuring one central core polytope surrounded by eye-covered rings; the rings can be spherated circles, spheres, glomes, etc. and vary in many aspects

Toriphans have a central polytope as opposed to the central eye of an Arraphim. The polytope is known as the core, and is always symmetrical to a certain degree, but usually is regular, uniform, or the dual of a uniform. The polytope is surrounded by overlapping eye-covered rings which are constantly rotating. The eyes on these rings are functional and the only vision-sensing parts of the Toriphan, though there are a lot of them on even one ring, especially if the ring's base hypersphere is beyond a circle.

The rings may be shaped like any hypertorus except hardened into corners and thinned, in such a way that a 3D torus would have thin rectangle cross sections. The "outer" side is always covered in eyes but the "inner" side may or may not be. The eyes can be one color or have all kinds of types of heterochromia, from alternating, to patterned, to different colors on different rings, or even completely random.

The core has similar qualities to that of the Symbolacron archetype. One facet opens into a mouth/vocal tract, while the other opens into the waste-expelling end, and the core contains all of the internal organs.

Also similar to Symbolacra, the rings are bound to the core by an invisible, incredibly strong force. The force does not block things from passing between the rings and the core, nor does such an occurence interrupt the force or harm the Omnigeon.

Toriphans have telekinesis for picking up and using objects. As for walking, they just roll in any direction in any subset of the six rotational planes, though double-rotation takes more energy than single-rotation since it requires both propulsion engines (a pair of core organs) to be activated rather than just one.

It's possible, though rare, for a Toriphan form to have no working facilities to manually reroll their genetics, similar to most Arraphim forms.

About 10% of forms are Toriphanic.

5.3.1.1.4:: Arraphim, featuring one central eye connected to multiple arrays of wings

Arraphim is the name of an extremely rare archetype of Omnigeon forms.

Arraphims have one central eye attached to multiple arrays of wings. These arrays are typically multidimensional and may be arranged in a rectangle, disk, hexagon, icosahedron, hexadecachoron, or really any other sufficiently symmetric shape.

The wings can range from angelic, to demonic, to insect-like, and the eye can have any iris color, and occasionally different sclera colors. The wings are nearly constantly in motion.

Arraphims typically levitate a few meters off the ground, hovering around instead of walking. They are capable of telepathy and telekinesis. Arraphims lack the biological facilities to produce an actual voice or grip objects, so they use exclusively the telepathy to communicate and telekinesis to pick up and use objects. Arraphims tend to have an unusually high tolerance for radiation. This means that Arraphims are typically stuck this way until radiation hits them in exactly the right way to cause a spontaneous reroll of form. Because of this, Arraphims often spend a significant amount of time under intense radiation to try and force this to happen, though it will usually take quite a while.

Thaneophyros, one of the overseers of the E3/Tuxe simulation, is stuck as an Arraphim.

About 0.00001% (one hundred thousandth) of forms are Arraphimic, however this is an average among everyone, and in reality the figure is that a few lineages with particularly chaotic forms are absolutely full of Arraphims, while almost nobody else is. Thaneophyros is in one of these Arraphimoid lineages, which also gives them a tendency to have crazy limb arrangements, large wings, and, more recently, a quintilaterally symmetric dragon merman form with built-in pocket universes and wireless muscle connections.

5.3.1.1.4.1:: Trivia
  • Arraphims are a generalization of an attempt to logically generalize biblically accurate seraphims into higher dimensions (seraphims normally have 6 wings, which could be seen as being arranged in 2 one-dimensional arrays each containing 3 wings)
5.3.1.2:: Reconfiguration

When an Omnigeon reconfigures, their brain and digestive tract do not reconfigure with everything else unless they are severely damaged or they are specifically exposed to the radiation from inside. Thus, their personality and intelligence stay mostly intact, other than possible personality changes due to their new body's capabilities, their opinion on it, and the occasional feeling of freedom from reconfiguration. Their voice also doesn't change, except in the very rare circumstance of becoming an Arraphim in which case the digestive tract is deleted and unnecessary; in this case, after the likely very lengthy process of breaking the lock to reconfigure, a new digestive tract is generated that comes with a new voice.

Due to the constant reforms, Omnigeons don't typically die of old age or physical injury, but they technically can, the former from running out of of the plentiful reconfigurations, and the latter from failing to be exposed to radiation quick enough to reset the injuries. They also aren't invulnerable and can die in various ways, most of them equivalent to causes of death on Earth. There is a limit of roughly 5000-5500 reconfigurations before the body kind of just crumbles, however this leads into new concepts called cycles and math agents.

5.3.2:: Phykommatons

Phykommatons are a species similar and related to Omnigeons, but with a different set of forms. They are genetically predisposed to be more outgoing and kind than Omnigeons, but this isn't a constant (there are very mean-spirited Phykommatons and very friendly Omnigeons). They inhabit geometries in The Stack that are the self-products of hyperbolic spaces, such as H2xH2, H3xH3, H4xH4, etc. There is also a more primitive and less intelligent variety existing in the 3D geometry of H2xE1.

Phykommatons, like Omnigeons, look completely different to one another. This is due to them possessing immense sensitivity to radiation. Upon being even slightly irradiated, their body will be completely and irreversibly reconfigured. Phykommatons are quite a lot more sensitive than Omnigeons, but compensate by having around 70000-80000 reconfigurations per cycle instead of 5000-5500. A specific, temporary configuration is called a "form". Forms can in theory be anything, but like Omnigeons, they almost always fall into a few archetypes, but they aren't named in human languages, so this article will give their names in an evolved form of the Quatrammotile language.

  • Qamqpegac look somewhat similar to the monster Campe from Greek myths, though they were injected into the myths of the simulated 3D human universe, not the other way around, and most of the monstrous lore was invented by humans.

    They feature an upper body with a torso, a varying number and arrangement of arms, and a head. The head usually has a lot of appendages where the hair would normally be; these may resemble snakes but don't have to, though they are usually always in motion no matter what. Their lower body is much larger and is typically either centaur-like, with more legs than arms on the upper body, or aquatic, with fish-like scales and a simple arrangement of one or more tails. In both cases there will be an additional scorpion-like tail. Large wings also protrude from the waist (these can be angelic, insectlike, dragonlike, or many other types), as well as several extra heads that differ from the main head.

    The "hair" and "waist heads" are conscious, but not separate from the main person; they all share a distributed, parallel consciousness. The upper body can vary from humanlike to draconic.
  • Aeduraegac have multiple heads (not necessarily 7) in some sort of symmetrical arrangement, as well as a lower body with at least 2^(dimension - 1) legs arranged in another symmetrical arrangement (so quadripedal in 3D, octapedal in 4D, 16-pedal in 5D, etc.). They were the inspiration for hydras in Greek myths. Their form usually varies from snake-like to draconic, but can occasionally deviate further. Aeduraegac have the ability to duplicate their heads, but they always duplicate in some sort of symmetrical, extendable pattern, which varies by the individual. Unlike the Greek myths, this is at will and not when heads are cut off.

    Their consciousness is distributed amongst the heads in a kind of parallel processing fashion.
  • Nrenqqecegac, from the waist up, are much like Brachiocrus Omnigeons, with a symmetrical arrangement of arms, a head ranging from human-like to draconic, occasionally going wolf-like, cat-like, or other. However, from the waist down, they have leaves and roots, which can be controlled. They aren't rooted into the ground and can still walk, but it can be awkward as the roots have to constantly grip surfaces and release as they move forward, thus they are somewhat immobile on slippery surfaces such as ice and glass, and may get their roots stuck in thick mud. Luckily, if stuck, they can usually reroll their genetics into some other form, though like some forms of Omnigeons, Nrenqqecegac are sometimes unable to manually reroll and resistant to radiation, meaning they're stuck in their current form for a while. In this case, these individuals often spend a significant amount of time under intense radiation to try and force a reroll to happen, though it will usually take quite a while.
  • Qwthaphegac are the most "normal" of the Phykommaton archetypes, though they're still a bit weird by human standards. They have a reptilian appearance with colorful metallic plates, and a large tail. Much like the upper bodies of Qamqpegac and Nrenqqecegac, as well as Brachiocrus Omnigeons, they have a symmetrical arrangement of arms and legs. They typically have bilateral symmetry, but this may be trilateral, quadrilateral, quintilateral, or higher in less common cases. They have a visor-like eye configuration with an LED display made purely out of biological cells. Their feet are digitigrade and they always have weird thin horns behind their visor.
  • Chatqaibhegac's bodies have no internal organs, no vocal tracts, and no eyes. They communicate via telepathy, use objects via telekinesis, levitate instead of walking, and have "intrinsic vision". The intrinsic vision sees all angles around them as if it were projected to the inside of a hypersphere. By humans who know about the multiverse of non-Euclidean and higher dimension geometries known as The Stack, they are sometimes jokingly called Startesian Products.

    Their bodies are the Cartesian product of the surfaces of two uniform star polytopes, made out of biological cells. This is different from a typical Cartesian product. While normally the Cartesian product of two squared would be a 4D tesseract, the product of the surfaces inflates one by the other, producing a 3-dimensional torus with a square path and square cross-sections. 5.4:: History
5.4.1:: Basics

Since every world in the Stack has its own timezone, and every time manifold has its own entire concept of time, times will be measured relative to Uncovesseltuxe, in ~SL(2,R) x E ;; E1 ;; E0 years (at the current rate, one of these years is about 356 human days and 4 human hours, with each year being 140 of his days and each of his days being 61 of his hours).

The E1-timespaced portions of the Stack are only 3,500,000 years old in the canonical present that the novel takes place. However, there is a canonical future after the novel which takes place later than that. Up until 950,000 years ago, the stack was mostly Thyngeoids, plants, and immortal creatures created by evolution. However, 950,000 years ago, the concept of "death" was deliberately created and bioengineered into some organisms to give people a lot of time to exercise math agent control without worrying about normal life affairs. It could occur in many different ways, but no matter how it occurred, one would be reborn after a deliberate length of 1.2 million years of meta-time in the relative frame of reference to their time experience in life. This rebirth timeout was not ever written down and was quickly forgotten by newer generations. At the time of the novel, the concept of death simply isn't old enough for a rebirth to have occurred.
